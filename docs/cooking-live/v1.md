1) Screen Structure (Mobile First)
Zone A - Status + Clock Band
Sticky top band above main content, below safe-area inset.

Contains: elapsed timer, total remaining, active heat zone, protein temp, and confidence signal ("On track", "Late 35s", "Ahead 20s").

Zone B - Current Step Command Card
Largest object on screen; only one command sentence in bold.

Contains: action verb, countdown ring, zone chip (Direct/Indirect), and one contextual hint line. Tap area spans entire card for gloves/dirty hands.

Zone C - Next Step Preview
Single-line prefetch of next action with T-minus chip.

Example: "In 00:35, then flip ribeye and move to indirect zone."

Zone D - Timeline Rail + Controls
Compact horizontal timeline and two thumb-reachable controls.

Controls: "Mark done" (primary), "Delay 30s" (secondary). Bottom nav always remains visible and untouched.

Interaction Ceiling
During active cooking, no screen requires typing. Live mode uses tap-only and long-press confirm patterns to avoid accidental triggers with greasy fingers.
2) Runtime Flow (Start to Finish)
Entry sequence (first 12 seconds)
0-2s: hero "Cook starts now" transition with setup visual + zone badges.

2-6s: first command card expands; countdown ring starts immediately.

6-12s: next-step preview fades in; timeline rail snaps to step 1 of N.

In-flight step loop (repeats each step)
1) Current step dominates screen with one imperative action.

2) At T-20s, pre-alert state appears in amber and next step becomes explicit.

3) At 0s, auto-transition banner appears with two outcomes: auto-advance or "Need more time?".

4) User can confirm done in one tap; if no tap for 8s, step auto-advances with a "continuous mode" marker.

Completion sequence
Final cook step resolves into rest mode (large rest countdown + no urgency color).

End state shows carve/readiness card and one CTA: "Save this cook profile" for retention.

3) Core Components (Ship Spec)
Current Step Card
Primary line format:

Verb + object + zone, max 52 chars. Example: "Sear ribeye side A on direct heat."

Secondary line:

Single tactical cue only. Example: "Do not move meat until timer ends."

Timer behavior:

Circular countdown + numeric mm:ss. Ring thickens in final 15s for urgency.

Next Step Preview
Always visible during active cook.

Contains only three fields: T-minus, action verb, destination zone.

At T-20s transitions from muted to high-contrast to prime motor response.

Timeline Bar
Live Step Rail

Now: Sear side A (3:00)

Next: Flip + move indirect

Segment states: completed (success tone), current (accent), upcoming (neutral), delayed (warning hatch).

Timer + Alerts System
Alert cadence is fixed and predictable:

T-60 subtle pulse, then T-20 amber pre-alert, then T-05 high-contrast flash, then 0s step handoff.

No modal interruptions. Alerts are layered inside existing layout zones.

6) UX Principles that Force Flow State
One command at a time

Every screen moment has one dominant sentence. No competing paragraphs or stacked choices.

Predictable tempo

Alert rhythm repeats identically across steps, so users internalize cadence and trust the assistant.

Progress visibility

Timeline always communicates how far they are from serving, turning waiting into momentum.

Low-friction recovery

"Delay 30s" and auto-advance prevent failure spirals; the app adapts instead of blaming.

7) Why This Beats Traditional Cooking Apps
Navigation, not documentation
Recipe apps expose all instructions at once. Parrillero Pro reveals only what matters now and orchestrates timing in real time.

Fast feedback + confidence
Each completed step gives immediate progression cues, shrinking anxiety and increasing mastery. Users return because the cook feels controlled and professional.

North-star outcome
After two sessions, users should trust Live Mode more than memory. The product wins when they feel under-assisted without it.
